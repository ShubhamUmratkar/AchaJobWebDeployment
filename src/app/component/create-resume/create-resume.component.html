Tips for Building a Strong Resume

1. Start with a Clear Format

  • Use a clean, professional layout with consistent font sizes and styles.
  • Prioritize readability; use bullet points instead of long paragraphs.
  • Stick to a one-page resume unless you have extensive experience.

2. Tailor Your Resume to Each Job

  • Customize your resume for each application.
  • Highlight skills and experiences relevant to the specific role.
  • Use keywords from the job description to pass Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS).

3. Write a Compelling Professional Summary

  • Keep it brief (2–3 sentences).
  • Focus on your unique value proposition, key skills, and career goals.

4. Quantify Achievements

  • Use numbers, percentages, or tangible outcomes to demonstrate your impact.
  • Example: “Increased sales by 25% in six months by implementing a new CRM system.”

5. Highlight Relevant Skills

  • List both technical and soft skills, tailored to the job requirements.
  • Avoid generic terms like “hardworking” unless backed by examples.

6. Use Action Verbs

  • Start bullet points with strong action verbs such as "managed," "developed," "led," "implemented," etc.

7. Prioritize Content

  • Place the most relevant and impactful information near the top.
  • List experiences in reverse chronological order (most recent first).

8. Include Relevant Keywords

  • Incorporate terms from the job description to improve ATS compatibility.

9. Avoid Overloading with Information

  • Focus on quality over quantity; highlight the most relevant achievements.
  • Limit work experience to the last 10–15 years, unless older roles are highly relevant.

10. Proofread and Edit Thoroughly

  • Check for grammar, spelling, and formatting errors.
  • Have a trusted friend or colleague review your resume for feedback.

11. Keep Design Professional

  • Avoid overly fancy designs unless applying for a creative role.
  • Use simple fonts like Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman.

12. Include a Cover Letter (if applicable)

  • Pair your resume with a tailored cover letter to explain your interest and suitability for the role.

13. Keep It Updated

  • Regularly update your resume with new skills, certifications, and accomplishments.
